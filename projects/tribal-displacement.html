Displaced Development explores the sad realities of tribal communities living in forests through satire

The Story in Watercolor and Sound

This motion graphics piece uses the soft, handmade charm of watercolor puppetry to explore a harsh reality: the displacement of India's tribal (Adivasi) communities from their ancestral forests for industrial development. Narratives are expressed through symbolic imagery, like a runaway marble representing the irreversible loss of leaving home.

The Sound

The soundscape creates conflict: serene forest sounds and a sarcastic narration that's disrupted by harsh city noise, ending with authentic Irula tribal music recorded in Bandipur forest.

A Voice from Bandipur

A conversation in 2019 with an Irula tribe member revealed their key pain point was being pushed from forests and forced to build their homes again. There was a lack of modern housing- pakka houses that 'don't bring in the rain.'

The True Cost of Progress

My conversation with the Irula tribe members revealed the true cost of "development": the elders are confined to the forest's edges, while the youth are pushed toward distant cities, each generation fractured in its search for a place to call home.
